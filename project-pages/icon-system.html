Icon system for Setu

At Setu, I contributed to defining a visual style, establishing guidelines for icons, and creating approximately 150 of them.

These pictograms proved to be incredibly versatile, finding their way into the website, sales decks, print collaterals like employee ID cards, and even Slack emojis representing company values.


Special thanks to Prajna for defining the style and Sujan for his guidance throughout the process.


Defining the purpose and scope

At Setu, the team always ensures that every piece we create together is crafted with care and attention to detail. We believe in subtle, stoic design. What we needed—


A set that adds clarity and serves as visual shortcuts for our website and pitch decks.

A clean, balanced style was needed that was easy on the eyes and adaptable for the future.

Given Setu's focus on finance and tech, we needed a balance of fun and seriousness.

We aimed to reinforce the brand identity, as these were among the first visual elements, aside from 3D renders.


Exploring design concepts

We began with a rough style and a fun goal—creating 8–10 icons for the team page which was in progress then. While creating the initial icons was quick and straightforward, designing finance-related ones required deeper thought. We had to account for overlaps with numerous financial terms and ensure they carried a distinctly Indian touch.


Establishing guidelines

With time, we decided on how to use the colours and elements to use, consistent corner radius, overlap between elements since it was colour-glass, we couldn’t have messed up with both clarity of thought and the visual. Also, not to forget, dark theme.


Going the extra mile

We went beyond simply creating the package in Figma. Instead, with a lot of help from Shekhar, Adi and Pankaj, we made the icons open source on Crucible, allowing everyone to easily copy them into slides and enabling people outside the organisation to customise them to match their brand colours.

Maintaining and evolving the system

After four years of refinements, it’s incredibly rewarding to see the icon set being used across all platforms and types of collaterals at Setu.


These icons have become an integral part of Setu’s identity.


I believe it’s important for a designer to feel fulfilled, to take pride in their work, and to still appreciate a project even months later. For me, the icon system became exactly that—a project that taught me patience.

At first glance, it may seem like just a set of icons, but the effort involved in conceptualising, designing, and, most importantly, overseeing its implementation on the design website—and seeing it adopted by other teams—was truly heartwarming.
